What Is Native Advertising?

Native advertising is a kind of advertising that takes the form of the advertising platform. In other words, it doesn’t feel or behave like a normal advertisement. Instead, it feels like a part of the platform on which it is displayed. As long as the sponsored piece of content is “in-feed,” and doesn’t disrupt the normal user experience of the platform, it can be referred to as “native” advertising.

According to Dictionary.com, Native Advertising is “content on a website that conforms to the design and format of the site and is integrated into the site’s usual content.”

The most common type of native advertising is editorial-based content, in similar subject and style to the rest of the content already on the platform. However, the label of native advertising can also be applied to other areas, like sponsored social media posts.

Native Advertising vs.
Display Advertising:
The Shift to Value-First

Traditional display advertising has been around for centuries, and easily made the transition to the digital world of the internet. Websites, platforms, and search engines have long sold traditional display advertising space on their pages as a way to generate revenue. And, advertisers realized a nice return on their investment.

However, over time, the phenomenon of “ad blindness” has taken over, which causes typical users to have to see an ad several times before they even notice it. Even in cases where users do notice the ads, the quality of engagement compared to the number of clicks has taken a steep drop.

Clicks are still there, but the same can’t be said of engagement.

As more advertisers flooded the display advertisement space, the more ad blindness became apparent. As ads became more common, they also became less effective.

The diverging graphs of decreased effectiveness and increased cost has led advertisers to try something else: value-first advertising.

What Is Value-First Native Advertising?

Value-first is an assumption that, if you give the reader something of value, they are more likely to respond positively toward your offer, or advertisement. This assumption has largely been proven valid, which has led larger publishers and advertisers to approach advertising through a new vehicle: sponsored content.

While sponsored content can take a lot of different forms, the purpose is to provide value through the content, which is used to drive engagement with the targeted offer. ‘Value,’ in the sponsored content approach, can be defined as anything that is educational, inspirational, or even entertaining.

7 Benefits of Sponsored Content Native Advertising

There are many benefits to sponsored content advertising over traditional display advertising.
These are four of the most apparent benefits of most native advertising campaigns:

1. Experience Higher Engagement Rates

According to a 2017 report in the HuffPost, native advertisements drive 20-60% more engagement than standard ads, with a 25% lift in users actively reading the advertisement over traditional advertisements. Perhaps the greatest statistic from the report is an 18% increase in purchase intent from those who engage with native advertisements.

2. Seems More Trustworthy

Entrepreneur magazine pointed out that the majority of users trust sponsored content more than traditional advertising. And the difference is getting wider as the public increasingly tires of traditional advertising methods.

Apple, for instance, released ad blockers in Safari web browser by default. Ad blocker apps are very popular, even among younger demographics. And thanks to modern streaming technology, commercials are being skipped.

The root of the matter is, most people dislike being sold to. Sponsored content is a way to bridge the gap, engage the audience on their terms, and elicit the proper response from those who are engaged and interested in the offer.

3. More Time to Communicate Value

As mentioned before, users have become good at not even noticing display advertisements. And when they do, it’s such a quick transaction; the ad only has a fraction of a second to make an appeal, and are limited to small micro-messages to drive engagement.

Sponsored content, on the other hand, drives greater audience retention, giving advertisers more time, and opportunity to elicit a response.

4. Advertising With Context

A display ad is quick and somewhat static. When the reader engages with your long-form content for 60 seconds or more, you have a much higher likelihood of inducing a response. In short, you can add context to the advertisement, which adds clarity to value, and increases user response.

5. Build Trust With Thought Leadership

Helpful content that adds value to the reader positions the brand as a thought leader and builds trust and credibility. With trust being vital to conversion rates, building trust on the front end of advertising removes friction from the rest of the conversion funnel.

6. Wider Appeal With Narrower Message

Long-form content gives adds flexibility over traditional advertising, giving brands more opportunities to creatively fine-tune their message. They can customize the message for specific segments of their market to increase the effectiveness of their sponsored content even more.

7. Thoughtful Content Has Viral Capabilities

Traditional ads are static in appeal. Helpful, long-form sponsored content is often shared by readers to their social networks, which increases the organic reach through “word of mouth,” which adds even more credibility to the content. Content that is shared has the potential to go viral, which could explode a brand’s exposure overnight.

4 Brands Winning With Native Content

Every major publisher in the world is engaged, in some way, with native content advertising, from Time and Forbes Magazines to the New York Times and the Wall Street Journal. Younger media companies, like Buzzfeed, are further pushing the popularity and development of native advertising.

The largest social media channels in the world are also driving native advertising monetization, such as Facebook, Instagram, and Tumblr. However, the following companies are mastering native content and using it to dramatically expanding their audience and drive engagement with their brand.

General Motors

1 / 4

When the Camaro Zl1 1LE was tested at the famous Nürburgring Nordschleife track, GM recognized the opportunity to engage their audience and increase buzz. Rather than creating advertisements for the vehicle, they decided to document the event.

“We sent a film crew to capture it all (in-car, aerial, interviews, driver prep, etc.),” said Whitney Drake. “Then we researched the hottest racing video games for inspiration and designed an interface to overlay the video, making it feel much more modern and interactive.”

They posted the video to YouTube and Facebook, and embedded the full video into a press release. The campaign resulted in more than 158 news stories and 1.6 million views of the video on Facebook alone.

“We knew the Camaro’s time would garner coverage, but through integrated storytelling and powerful assets, we transformed what could have been standard coverage into a powerful moment in car history for enthusiasts across the globe.” Drake said.

GoPro

4 / 4

And it’s working. CMI points out that GoPro’s YouTube channel has 3.2 million subscribers. Of their athlete partners’ videos, 388 of them have received more than 50 million views… each!

How To Implement Native Advertising Effectively

The elusive secret of effective native content lies in user engagement. You can create content for years, but it won’t matter if it’s not content your audience wants to engage with.

Creating content is hard. Engaging your target audience is even harder.

Effective native advertising is the marriage of two separate principles: interesting content and a deep understanding of the ideal audience for that content.

Felix Baumgartner dropping from space is interesting content, but doesn’t appeal to Allstate Insurance’s audience in a meaningful way. However, for the young adrenaline junkies of the world, those who consume the most Red Bull, the prospect of jumping from the edge of space is invigorating and inspiring. They not only want to be associated with the brand that did it, but they also want to do it themselves.

The sad truth is, for every GoPro and Airbnb, there are hundreds of companies struggling to get results from their native content advertising campaigns. However, with the right tools, you can get the insight to close the gap between interesting content and audience engagement.
